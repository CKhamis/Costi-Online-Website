2Ô∏è‚É£: Costi Productions 2015

In 2015, I made a very large amount of videos. I have reached a reconnaissance of video production in a way. I got access to Adobe CS5 and CS4 software packs, and I was self-teaching based on what I had learned from school and just trying things out. Each video would try out a new technique or idea and the next video would utilize what I had learned from before and add further experimentation. 2015 was home to many iconic videos such as Amal +.

Amal + Episode 2

Adobe Premiere Pro | 2015

This episode of Amal + utilized my previous finds from the first episode and built off of it with all-new segments that had far better quality. I also continued to experiment with video effects and strategies. For example, I tried keying the opacity of the green screen videos dynamically, changing the way I keyed out the green screen, and experimented with music videos (which Tina and Amal were very keen on trying).

Amal +

Adobe Premiere Pro | 2015

Amal + episode 1 was a series of unrelated video segments that played right after each other. The main idea of this video was to test and try out new ideas for videos. The discoveries I made when making this video were super integral to the videos I made afterwards. For example some things I tried in this video was to use a different color for the green screen, having multiple layers of video, green screen color correction, hue filters, and most importantly, backing up videos. At the time, I had stored all raw videos on my camera's SD card and when I ran out of space, I formatted the card, not understanding that my videos were being deleted. I only have an old version of the video that I thankfully exported earlier, however I will not be showing it here because the segments in it are a bit to weird. Amal + would eventually have 2 sequels.

1Ô∏è‚É£: Costi Inc. Era

The Costi Inc. Era was the very first stage in Cost Media's evolution. Initially starting in 2011 and ending in 2015, I used to make simple Flash animations, vector artwork, and short movies with iMovie. I found myself a portable version of Flash 8 and Fireworks back when they were owned by Macromedia and begun my work. It was during this time when I found a passion for creating media. I also bought myself a simple video editor to tinker with. Along with making my first animated logo, I had made the Terency show, a flash-based school project, begun working on a Flash game, and made a trailer for it.
