About Costi Online

In May 2022, I embarked on the development journey of Costi Online, a personal project conceived with the primary goal of showcasing my Minecraft worlds. Beyond this, it also served as a platform to rigorously test and hone my skills in various domains, including front-end and back-end development, hosting infrastructure, and user experience (UX) design.

Originally conceived as a companion website for my self-hosted Minecraft server, Costi Online has undergone a remarkable evolution, expanding its scope to encompass a wider array of topics and functionalities.

The genesis of this personal website was not without precedent. Prior attempts at crafting my own digital presence had been made during my coding journey, but none had reached a state of full development or readiness for deployment. It took approximately a month of dedicated effort to bring forth the inaugural publicly released version of Costi Online, which was initially focused exclusively on the Minecraft server.

Since then, the website has seen a continuous influx of features and improvements, with some changes being more substantial than others. Notably, the transition from an H2 database to MySQL marked a pivotal point in its development, enhancing its data handling capabilities. Additionally, the incorporation of an API has empowered the website with greater interactivity and flexibility. Furthermore, the entire platform now runs seamlessly on a self-hosted Linux machine, underscoring my commitment to managing every aspect of its infrastructure.

Costi Online draws inspiration not only from my prior work but also from various personal websites I've encountered over time. It stands as a testament to my dedication to ongoing self-improvement and my passion for presenting my technical skills and projects to the digital world.
